Say hello to our little (big) friend

May 7, 2014 /PD

During my second visit to Austin, RC and I packed in a lot of food and adventure. It was Cinco de Mayo weekend, so the town was bustling with activity. We checked out the Pecan street festival and watched one of the largest bat colonies fly out across the river from their home under a bridge. Later on, we went on an "urban hike" to get to the Odd Duck for dinner, where we ran into a small wildflower pathway leading to some pretty sweet biking courses. On Cinco de Mayo, we headed to Hamilton Pool. After waiting an hour to get into the park, we finally jumped into the freezing waters and swam through some waterfalls. RC felt like he may have been dying throughout the whole experience. Just as we were about to head out, we ran into our little friend, a cotton mouth water snake (later found out to be venomous), chilling right near the rock where we were rinsing off. Before leaving Austin, we made it to a few more food carts, including the famous Gourdough's! The trip had a bittersweet ending with my flight getting cancelled. I had to miss a day of work, but it was nice to spend another day in Austin!

Triple Black Diamond Threat

March 3, 2014 /PD

Dropbox hosted their annual trip to Lake Tahoe at the Northstar resort. As usual, they did not fail to impress. Custom made luggage tags, wristbands, and winter hats were only a few of the many perks, not to mention the hospitality houses. After a long bus ride on Friday, everybody headed to the notorious houses for a night to remember...or forget. They were stocked with everything you could think of to ensure a "good time". Saturday was spent skiing in the perfect weather, it was RC's first experience. After only a few (check out the video) wipeouts, he graduated from the bunny hill to a green slope. We managed to sneak our way on to a noon bus on Sunday, making it back to San Francisco in time to tour the Yerba Buena Art Center before catching red eyes back to Austin and Ft. Lauderdale.

Getting Weird in Austin

February 17, 2014 /PD

Austin just got a little weirder: food, food, and food are the 3 words that describe my visit to RC for Valentine's Day/long weekend, and also his first weekend in his new home of Austin, TX. We started off the weekend at the Barton Creek Greenbelt. Despite the name, it wasn't very green...we walked through the trails and had a relaxing picnic from the Live Oak Market on the rock ledges. Later that night we explored the food carts of Austin, enjoying some tapas and pretty amazing Indian food, ending the night with some Amy's ice cream. The next day, we ate some incredible carrot cake french toast for brunch. Finally, we made it to Whip In for some Indian-inspired Tex-Mex and live music. After all this food, it was time to visit some rescue cats at Petco, RC's favorite part of the whole trip!

The Real Ricardo

January 1, 2014 /PD

RC and I spent New Years Eve together in Chicago. After our train got delayed due to some snowy weather and mid trip power outages (don't worry, alcohol was allowed on board!), we finally made it to the city. After receiving some surprising special attention as we walked into The Shrine, we were directed to the VIP section of the club....what? Did we get upgraded? How long with this last?...well, about 10 minutes. Shortly after spilling food all over the VIP booth, the real "Ricardo" showed up (the club confused RC's name) and claimed his spot. We spent the rest of the night in the standing room only section where we belonged, listening to a mix of comedy and live music until sharing a champagne toast at midnight.

Wine Country and the Best Reggae Band Ever

September 21, 2013 /PD

I visited RC before moving to Florida to start my new job at Citrix. We visited the hipster version of wine country...a.k.a. Mendocino County. On our way there, we stopped in Healdsburg for dinner and had the best dessert I've tasted to date: chocolate creme brulee...check out the photo below, no words to describe. After getting drunk at a few wineries (don't worry, we had a craigslist driver who surprisingly didn't murder us), hanging out at Mendocino National Forest, kayaking through caves, and eating some chocolate covered insects, we headed back to San Francisco. After having some amazing eggplant at Kamdesh Afghan Kabab House, we unexpectedly happened upon the BEST REGGAE CONCERT EVER, at least according to the bouncer...lots of dancing, drinks, and saying no to drugs definitely made it a memorable night.
